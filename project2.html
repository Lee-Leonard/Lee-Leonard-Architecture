Proposal Overview

The Wind Walk is an innovative project designed to harness wind energy along Rathlin Island’s north coast. Extending scenic trails over the water, the modular wind harnessing system captures the Atlantic winds to generate sustainable power for the residents Rathlin Island whilst concurrently mitigating plastic sea pollutants. Suspended above the sea with flexible supports, the pathway features a net system that collects plastic waste, protecting marine life and haulting these polluntants before reaching the shore. Made from the recycled plastics it collects, the Wind Walk offers a sustainable solution that benefits both the community and environment. This project represents a vital step toward Rathlin Island’s sustainable future.

This initial concept idea is where both the ‘Wind Walk’ and ‘Resource Hub’ schemes originated. It outlines a visionary wind energy harnessing system, strategically positioned along Rathlin’s north coast. The aim was to integrate the system into the island’s cliff-side landscape while supporting a sustainable, self-sufficient future for the island. The proposal extends the scenic walking trails over the water, using ‘wind walls’ to harness energy for the people of Rathlin Island to utilise this resource.

Design Approach

The Wind Walk developed into a walkable pathway extending over the water, continuing to utilise the wind walls as barriers to harness the potential of the Atlantic winds. The structure is suspended above the waters surface using airtight water booms and supported by a custom-designed frame and swivel system that secures the modular construct in place while allowing for flexibility and durability. An integrated net system beneath the pathway collects and filters plastic waste, protecting marine wildlife and preventing contaminants from reaching the shore. Both the pathway and wind walls are constructed entirely from recycled plastics that are captured, processed, and repurposed-offering an innovative, sustainable solution that benefits both the residents and the natural environment of Rathlin Island.

As night falls over Rathlin Island, the Wind Walk comes to life - lined with discreet, waterproof lighting that softly illuminates the pathway. Powered entirely by the energy harnessed from the wind walls, this gentle glow extends the usability of the Wind Walk well into the evening, offering residents and visitors a unique night-time experience. The wind walls continue to operate around the clock, silently spinning on their central axes, capturing the relentless Atlantic breeze. With their constant motion and renewable energy output, they hold the enduring potential to transform life on Rathlin Island - sustainably and for the better.

Usable day and night, the Wind Walk becomes a vital part of Rathlin Island’s infrastructure, offering sustainable energy and environmental protection. Designed with dampeners and flexible components, it adapts to changing sea conditions, ensuring continuous operation. When the plastic-collecting nets are full, tugboats can remove the waste without disrupting the structure’s position or function.
